Book Reviews

On the Road-Jack Kerouac

Review: "On the Road" is Jack Kerouac's iconic Beat Generation novel about freedom, adventure, and the American spirit.

My Thoughts: "On the Road" is unique for its stream-of-consciousness prose, vivid portrayal of the Beat Generation, and its exploration of freedom and self-discovery. It captures the restless, rebellious spirit of post-WWII America, influencing literature and culture profoundly.

Poor Economics-Banerjee

Review: "Poor Economics" by Abhijit Banerjee and Esther Duflo explores the lives of the world's poor, challenging common misconceptions and offering insights into poverty alleviation through rigorous economic research.

My Thoughts: "Poor Economics" is unique for its groundbreaking approach, blending field experiments with economic theory to understand poverty. It provides practical, evidence-based solutions, humanizing the poor and transforming global development policy.
